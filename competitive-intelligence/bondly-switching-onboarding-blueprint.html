Keep the spine — sharpen the edges, and change one belief. The Verdict — read this page if you read nothing else

The edge: own the switch before MortgageMarket does — honestly.

No South African player leads with a savings-first, multi-bank, no-hard-credit-check switching journey for existing homeowners. The originators are purchase-led (BetterBond explicitly won't do simple rate-switching); the banks' switch pages are single-lender and form-first. That gap is real, and Bondly is already standing in it. But it is a 6–12 month window, not a moat — so the recommendation is to move fast on five sharpening moves and one reframe, while building the one asset that compounds: the nurture list.

The five things that decide this
  • Biggest opportunity — a multi-bank, savings-first switch funnel is uncontested in SA. Bondly can own "switch, let banks compete, we handle everything."
  • Biggest threat — MortgageMarket just won direct origination contracts with all banks (May/Jun '26) + 2-min Experian pre-qual. The window is now.
  • The honesty fix that is also the growth lever — show net savings after switching costs + break-even months on the Verdict. It's a CPA s.41 duty and it sorts visitors into "switch now" vs "watch."
  • Kill fear-of-rejection — reframe the Switch Score as bankability, earned by a soft non-pull signal (LTV + affordability band), not an empty promise.
  • Get one number first — the bank→originator commission on a switch (+ clawback on early cancellation). It decides how hard to push funnel vs nurture.
DO Ship first
  • Net-of-cost Verdict (break-even + loss-framing + "your new bank may cover R10k–R30k").
  • Compliant rate-watch opt-in (POPIA s.69) keyed to prime moves + LTV milestones.
  • "What Bondly does for you" admin-disappears checklist.
DON'T Ship yet
  • The "3+ banks will compete" claim with zero credit signal — CPA s.41 exposure.
  • SA ID capture before formal-application intent — POPIA data-minimisation.
  • Treating the funnel as the whole product — the median bond can't switch profitably today.
"Most of your visitors shouldn't switch this year — and that is the business, not the problem."
The break-even math (R30k–R40k friction vs ~R300/mo saved on a small rate gap) means the volume play is "watch & switch later." Build the list; convert the qualified minority now. Full reasoning on p.8.
The one big call

Reframe Bondly as a switch-readiness engine. The Verdict screen becomes a sorting machine: it qualifies and converts the high-value "switch-now" segment (large bonds, big rate gaps, low LTV — where a new bank covers most costs), and it captures everyone else into a compounding, compliant rate-watch list that re-engages on prime cuts and equity milestones. That single change turns your structural weakness (thin switch-now inventory) into your durable moat (a proprietary, intent-rich audience no competitor can copy by cloning your funnel).

The Verdict, continued — strategy & ranked moves

Three reframes, then a ranked build order.

R1
Product = engine, not funnel
Median sub-R1.5m bond: ~R30–40k friction ÷ ~R300/mo saving on a 0.5% cut = 8–10yr break-even. So "switch now" is the value business (big bonds, ≥1% gaps, low LTV); "watch & switch later" is the volume business. The rate-watch list is co-equal, not a footnote.
R2
Earn the bankability claim
You can't honestly say "3+ banks will compete" with no data — that's a CPA s.41 misleading-representation risk. Keep "no hard credit check," but compute a soft non-pull signal (LTV from balance ÷ est. value, affordability band, arrears self-declaration) and calibrate the claim to it.
R3
It's a window, not a wall
Funnel mechanics are replicable — MortgageMarket could bolt a switch flow onto direct bank contracts in a quarter, with a credit signal. Bondly's durable edge is switch-specific positioning + CX craft + the compounding nurture asset + "we make the admin disappear."

Findings 3 — the CX & behavioural evidence

Why this ordering, and not another.

The principles doing the work
  • Value before friction. Show the savings range + Switch Score before any personal ask. Never gate the Verdict — the result is the reason they continue.
  • Field count drives abandonment, not steps. Multi-step beats single-page (~86% higher completion in lead-gen); 27% abandon a form that merely looks long. Split the asks; show progress.
  • Endowed progress. A bar that starts at "Step 1 complete" (because the calculator was real work) lifts completion vs one starting at zero.
  • Loss aversion. "You're paying R1,850 more every month" beats "you could save R1,850" — losses motivate ~2×.
  • Phone is the highest-friction field. ~70% are reluctant; making it optional has doubled completion. Email first, phone optional & late.
  • Ranges beat false precision. "R1,600–R2,100/mo" reads as honest; "R1,847" reads as invented. Keep the range; explain its basis.
  • Anxiety is per-field. Put reassurance next to the scary field (ID, income), not in a generic top banner. ID gets its own screen.
Recommended capture moment
Capture email the instant the Verdict lands — intent is at its peak and email is the lowest-anxiety ask. Defer phone (optional) and ID (gated behind apply-intent) to the committed end of the ladder, where sunk-cost makes them small asks. This is the difference between "more leads, lower quality" (capture early) and "fewer, bank-ready leads" (capture value-first) — and for a commission-on-completion model, quality wins.
Already doing well — protect these
  • Verdict before contact capture ✓
  • "Reading" animation primes credibility ✓
  • Savings as a range ✓
  • "Lower payment vs finish sooner" choice ✓
  • Optional (not mandatory) statement upload ✓
  • Staged capture via correlationId ✓
The tension to manage — frictionless vs bank-ready

The whole ladder optimises for completion. But the only revenue event is a bank accepting and the bond registering — so a flood of unscreened leads burns the bank relationships that are the actual moat. Resolution: keep the ladder frictionless, but compute a soft, non-pull bankability signal early (LTV + affordability band + arrears self-declaration) so the "banks will compete" claim is earned and the leads you submit are genuinely bank-ready. If acceptance rates still lag, a light Experian-style soft check (MortgageMarket's model) becomes the next lever — added as an optional accelerator, never a gate.

Findings 4 — the economics that reframe the product

The break-even math is the strategy.

Switching a bond in SA is a full re-registration, not a rate tweak. The cost hurdle is why "switch now" is a minority sport — and why the rate-watch list is the real volume business.

R30k–R40k
Realistic switching friction on a ~R1m bond (registration + cancellation + initiation)
~20 mo
Break-even on a typical 0.75–1% cut (R22k cost ÷ ~R1,100/mo saved)
8–10 yr
Break-even on a thin 0.5% cut on a small bond — i.e. "don't switch, watch"
Who should switch now (the value segment)
  • Larger bonds (≈ R1.5m+) where a 0.5–1% cut is real money.
  • Big rate gaps (1%+ above what they'd get today).
  • Low LTV / seasoned bonds — banks compete hardest and cover the most cost (Absa up to R30k at the top tier).
  • Long remaining horizon in the home (clears break-even comfortably).
SA's market is shifting to the >R3m band — exactly this segment. That's where to spend acquisition.
Who should watch (the volume segment)
  • Sub-R1.5m bonds with a modest rate gap — break-even too long today.
  • High-LTV or recently-originated bonds.
  • Anyone where the honest net-of-cost number says "not yet."
The reframe: these aren't lost leads — they're the compounding asset. A prime cut or a few years of paydown flips them to "switch now," and Bondly is the brand that told them honestly and kept watch.

Findings 5 — compliance & data (NCA · CPA · POPIA · FICA)

It's not FAIS. Know the real rules.

Home loans are NCA credit agreements, not FAIS "financial products" — so a savings comparison/Switch Score needs no FSP licence (FAIS only bites if you bundle insurance). Bondly is an intermediary, not a credit provider, so no NCR registration is required. The binding constraints are CPA (honesty), POPIA (consent), and the bank's own duties. Practitioner checklist — get formal legal sign-off before launch.

  • Share data with banks before explicit confirmation.
  • Use the rate-alert consent to send general marketing.

FICA KYC is the bank's obligation (it is the Accountable Institution), not Bondly's — but handle any ID/address you do collect securely under POPIA. Get a FAIS opinion only if you ever attach insurance to the flow.
